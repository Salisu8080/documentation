# Day 1 Training Plan: AI Foundations & Teaching Applications
**Theme:** Demystifying AI & Building Classroom Superpowers

## 🌅 Morning Session: AI Foundations & Prompt Engineering (09:00 - 12:30)

| Time | Activity | Details |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **09:00 - 09:30** | **Arrival & Setup** | Ensure everyone has internet access and at least one AI tool open (ChatGPT, Gemini, or Claude). |
| **09:30 - 09:45** | **🧊 ICE BREAKER 1** | **"Two Truths and an AI Lie"**<br>1. Ask participants to write 3 statements about themselves (2 true, 1 false).<br>2. *The Twist:* They must ask an AI tool to "Write 3 interesting facts about a university lecturer in [Their Field], one of which is a lie."<br>3. Share results. Did the AI come up with a believable lie? Did it stereotype their field?<br>**Goal:** Highlights AI's ability to hallucinate/invent convincing text immediately. |
| **09:45 - 10:30** | **Core Concepts** | **The Mental Model:** AI as a prediction engine, not a knowledge base.<br>**Tool Landscape:** When to use what (ChatGPT vs. Claude vs. Perplexity).<br>*Activity:* "Explore & Compare" (Slide 3) - Ask the same question to two different AIs. |
| **10:30 - 11:00** | **The Framework** | **The 5-Component Prompt:** Role, Context, Task, Format, Constraints.<br>*Activity:* "Prompt Transformation" (Slide 5) - Rewrite weak prompts ("Help me with research") into strong ones. |
| **11:00 - 11:15** | **☕ Tea Break** | |
| **11:15 - 12:15** | **Power Techniques** | **Deep Dive:** Few-Shot, Chain-of-Thought, and Role-Based prompting.<br>*Lab:* "Technique Practice" (Slide 10) - Apply all 3 techniques to one teaching task and one research task. |
| **12:15 - 12:30** | **Q&A / Wrap-up** | Review the morning's key wins. Transition to afternoon application. |

## ☀️ Afternoon Session: AI for Teaching & Content Creation (13:30 - 16:30)

| Time | Activity | Details |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **13:30 - 13:45** | **🧊 ICE BREAKER 2** | **"The Impossible Student"**<br>1. Ask AI to "Create a persona of a difficult student who asks impossible questions about [Participant's Subject]." <br>2. Have participants pair up and try to answer the "student's" question using AI to help them explain it simply.<br>**Goal:** Fun, low-stakes practice in using AI for explanation and adapting to a specific audience. |
| **13:45 - 14:30** | **Curriculum Design** | **Lesson Planning:** Generating comprehensive lesson plans with timing and activities (Slide 3).<br>**Assessments:** Creating MCQs, rubrics, and answer keys (Slide 5). |
| **14:30 - 15:15** | **Content Creation** | **Slide Outlines:** Generating structure and talking points for lectures (Slide 6).<br>**Differentiation:** "The Multi-Level Explanation" (Slide 9) - Explaining one concept to a 5-year-old, an undergrad, and a PhD student. |
| **15:15 - 15:30** | **🥤 Stretch Break** | |
| **15:30 - 16:15** | **The "Package"** | **Capstone Activity:** "Build a Complete Teaching Package" (Slide 7).<br>Participants assemble a full unit (Lesson Plan + Slides + Quiz) for *next week's class*. |
| **16:15 - 16:30** | **Day 1 Closing** | **Feedback Loop:** How to use AI to grade/give feedback on student work (Slide 10).<br>**Homework:** "Try one thing we built today in your real class prep." |
