# Day 2 Training Plan: Research, Ethics & Workflow
**Theme:** Rigor, Integrity & The Future of Work

## 🌅 Morning Session: AI for Research & Writing (09:00 - 12:30)

| Time | Activity | Details |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **09:00 - 09:30** | **Arrival & Recap** | Quick review of Day 1. Ensure everyone has access to a "Research-grade" tool (Elicit/Perplexity/Consensus - or at least understands the difference). |
| **09:30 - 09:45** | **🧊 ICE BREAKER 3** | **"The Hallucination Hunt"**<br>1. Give participants a fake citation: "Zubairu, S. (2024). *The Impact of AI on Nigerian Jollof Rice Pricing*. Journal of Culinary Economics."<br>2. Ask them to use AI to "find the abstract" for this paper.<br>3. Discuss: Did any AI try to invent an abstract? Did any correctly say "I can't find it"?<br>**Goal:** Viscerally demonstrate why you *never* trust AI citations without verification. |
| **09:45 - 10:30** | **The Research Workflow** | **Discovery vs. Generation:** Using tools to *find* papers (Lit Review) vs. *summarize* them.<br>**The Golden Rule:** "Never cite what you haven't read." (Slide 2)<br>*Activity:* "Research Question Refinement" (Slide 3) - Transforming a broad topic into a specific, researchable question using the FINER criteria. |
| **10:30 - 11:15** | **Literature Review** | **The Multi-Tool Approach:** <br>1. Elicit/Consensus to find papers.<br>2. Claude/ChatGPT to summarize themes *across* abstracts.<br>*Activity:* Participants perform a rapid "mini lit review" on their topic. |
| **11:15 - 11:30** | **☕ Tea Break** | |
| **11:30 - 12:30** | **Academic Writing** | **Drafting vs. Editing:** Using AI as a "Reviewer 2" (Critique) rather than a ghostwriter.<br>*Lab:* "Improve Your Abstract." Participants take an old abstract (or write a rough new one) and ask AI to "Critique this against high-impact journal standards." |

## ☀️ Afternoon Session: Ethics, Policy & Future Workflow (13:30 - 16:30)

| Time | Activity | Details |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **13:30 - 13:45** | **🧊 ICE BREAKER 4** | **"The Ethics Tribunal"**<br>1. Present a gray-area scenario: "A PhD student uses AI to polish every sentence of their thesis because English is their second language. Is this plagiarism?"<br>2. Participants vote: Green (Okay), Yellow (Caution), Red (Unethical).<br>3. Ask one person from each "color" to defend their vote.<br>**Goal:** Spark the debate for the Ethics session. There are no easy answers. |
| **13:45 - 14:30** | **Ethics & Policy** | **The Spectrum of Use:** Acceptable vs. Prohibited (Slide 2).<br>**Data Privacy:** What *never* goes into ChatGPT (student data, unpublished data) (Slide 4).<br>*Activity:* "Draft an AI Use Policy" (Slide 5) - Create a 1-page policy for their own department/course. |
| **14:30 - 15:15** | **Workflow Automation** | **The Prompt Library:** Stop typing from scratch. Build a personal toolkit.<br>*Activity:* "Build Your Toolkit" (Slide 8) - Create and save 5 reusable prompt templates (e.g., "The Lesson Planner," "The Peer Reviewer"). |
| **15:15 - 15:30** | **🥤 Stretch Break** | |
| **15:30 - 16:15** | **Peer Review & Sharing** | **Toolkit Swap:** (Slide 9) Pair up. Swap one prompt template. Test it. Give feedback.<br>**Goal:** Leave with 5 *tested* templates + 1 new one from a partner. |
| **16:15 - 16:30** | **Final Closing** | **The Future:** Where is this going next year? (Agents, Multimodal).<br>**Certificates / Closing Remarks.** |
