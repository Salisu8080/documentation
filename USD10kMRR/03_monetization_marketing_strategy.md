# PyLearn — Monetization & Marketing Strategy
## Target: $10,000 USD MRR by December 31, 2026

---

## The Core Strategic Problem

Before any tactics: PyLearn has 500 users and zero individual paying subscribers. The ₦5,000/month pricing failed entirely. The only revenue (₦550,000) came from two B2B organization deals.

This tells us three things:
1. **Nigerian B2C pricing psychology is a real barrier** — ₦5,000/month is the price of a decent meal out, but feels too high for a "study app"
2. **B2B (orgs) is the proven revenue channel** — two deals closed without formal marketing; this is where intent and budget exist
3. **The product activation and onboarding funnel is likely broken** — users sign up but don't experience enough value to consider paying

The path to $10,000 USD MRR requires attacking all three problems simultaneously: fix pricing, scale B2B aggressively, and repair the activation funnel.

**$10,000 USD/month at current exchange rate (~₦1,600/USD) = ₦16,000,000/month.**

## Pricing Strategy

### The Problem with Current Pricing
₦5,000/month is rational by global SaaS standards but fails in Nigeria because:
- It exceeds what most students can justify without experiencing clear value first
- There is no affordable entry point between ₦0 (free) and ₦5,000 (premium)
- The annual plan (₦50,000) is not prominently positioned as the better value choice

### Recommended New Tier Structure

#### Individual Tiers

| Plan | Price | Who it's for | Key pitch |
|---|---|---|---|
| Free | ₦0 | Students exploring; referral leads | The on-ramp |
| Premium Lite (new) | ₦1,500/month or ₦15,000/year | Nigerian students, NYSC corps members | Entry-level paid; affordable first step |
| Premium | ₦3,500/month or ₦35,000/year | Serious learners, career switchers | Full power at a reduced price |
| International/Diaspora | $7/month or $70/year | African diaspora, international learners | USD pricing, full premium features |

**Key decisions explained:**
- Remove the ₦5,000 price point as the only option — it created a cliff with no bridge
- ₦1,500/month is the "obviously affordable" tier — less than a Netflix subscription — that removes the psychological barrier
- ₦3,500/month replaces ₦5,000 as the standard premium; still meaningful revenue, much less resistance
- International USD pricing (via Paystack international cards) captures diaspora and global users at 2–3x the Nigerian price

#### Organization Tiers

| Plan | Price | Seats | Key features |
|---|---|---|---|
| Org Starter | ₦30,000/month | Up to 25 | Full premium access, basic reporting, org dashboard |
| Org Growth | ₦80,000/month | Up to 100 | Everything + CSV reports, deadline assignments, invoice generation |
| Org Enterprise | Custom (from ₦200,000/month) | Unlimited | Custom branding, white-label, dedicated support, SLA |

**Annual org contracts:** Offer 20% discount for 12-month prepayment — targeting ₦360,000–₦960,000 annual contracts.

## User Activation Fix (Do This First)

Before spending ₦1 on advertising, fix why 500 users converted at 0%. The most likely causes:

1. **No onboarding sequence** — users sign up and land on a dashboard with no guided first action
2. **No re-engagement emails** — users who don't return within 48 hours are silently lost
3. **The free tier doesn't create enough "aha moments"** before hitting a paywall
4. **No urgency or social proof** at the upgrade prompt

**Immediate fixes (1–2 weeks of dev time):**
- Add a 3-step onboarding checklist that appears for new users: (1) Complete your first lesson, (2) Try a challenge, (3) Check your rank
- Add automated emails at: 24 hours (encourage return), 3 days (re-engage), 7 days (last nudge with trial offer)
- Add a "Trial available — 7 days free" CTA to the nav bar for all free users during their first 30 days
- Add one testimonial + social proof number near the upgrade CTA

**Target:** Lift free-to-trial conversion from ~0% to 5–10% of active users.

**2. WhatsApp & Telegram Communities (Near-Zero CAC)**
WhatsApp is Nigeria's primary communication platform. NYSC groups, university study groups, and coding communities are active and share resources constantly.

Expected: 500–1,000 new signups in 90 days at near-zero cost.

## Conversion Funnel

### Stage 1: Awareness → Landing Page
- **Traffic sources:** Social media, Google, WhatsApp shares, referral links
- **Goal:** Visitor understands the product in <10 seconds
- **Optimization:** Add 3 social proof numbers to homepage: "500+ learners | 20+ tracks | 50+ achievements"
- **CTA:** "Start for free — no credit card needed"

### Stage 2: Landing → Signup
- **Current friction:** Form asks for phone + country — make this optional at signup (capture after first lesson)
- **Goal:** Signup in under 60 seconds
- **Optimization:** Add Google/social OAuth; reduce required fields

### Stage 3: Signup → Activation (First Value Moment)
- **Definition of activation:** User completes 1 lesson AND 1 challenge within first session
- **Current state:** No guided onboarding; likely high drop-off
- **Fix:** 3-step onboarding checklist; auto-select the "Python Fundamentals" track as the starting recommendation; show XP earned after first lesson with celebration animation

### Stage 4: Activation → Habit (Day 3–14)
- **Goal:** User builds a 7-day streak
- **Levers:** Daily mission system; streak reminder notifications; WhatsApp reminder option
- **Milestone email at Day 7:** "You've been learning for 7 days — you're 10x more likely to complete this track than the average learner"

### Stage 5: Habit → Trial
- **Trigger:** AI limit hit, premium track lock, or 14-day mark for active users
- **Offer:** 7-day free trial — no credit card required
- **Urgency:** "Your trial expires in X days" countdown in nav bar

### Stage 6: Trial → Paid
- **Window:** 7 days to convert
- **Actions during trial:**
  - Day 1: "Welcome to Premium — here's what just unlocked" email
  - Day 5: "2 days left on your trial" push notification
  - Day 7: "Your trial ends today — lock in your progress" email with upgrade CTA
- **Target conversion rate:** 30–40% of trial starts

### Stage 7: Paid → Retained
- **Churn prevention:** Monthly progress report email; streak preservation; new content announcements
- **Expansion:** Referral programme, annual upgrade offer at 3-month mark, org upsell for power users who mention their employer

## B2B Sales Approach

### Identifying Targets
1. Google: "Python bootcamp Lagos/Abuja/Port Harcourt" → 50 schools
2. LinkedIn: Search "L&D Manager Nigeria," "Head of Training Nigeria," "CS Department Nigeria"
3. Twitter: Follow and engage with #NigerianStartups, #EdTechNigeria, #TechInNigeria
4. NYSC: Each state has an SAED coordinator — there are 36 state coordinators nationally
5. Andela, Decagon, Semicolon, AltSchool — known tech education companies

### The Pitch
Frame PyLearn as a cost-reduction and quality improvement tool, not just "a course":
- "Replace your expensive LMS with a platform built for African learners at ₦80,000/month for 100 users"
- "Your instructors spend 40% of time answering basic questions — our AI tutor handles those automatically"
- "Track every student's progress in real time — no more guessing who's falling behind"

### Closing Process
1. LinkedIn/email outreach → 3-day follow-up if no response
2. Free 30-day trial offer (up to 20 users, no credit card)
3. 20-min demo call: Show battles mode, org dashboard, progress reports
4. Post-trial: Share progress report showing student engagement data
5. Close on Org Starter or Growth plan; ask for 12-month upfront for 20% discount

### Target Org Pipeline
- Month 1–2: 5 orgs on free trial simultaneously
- Month 3: 3–4 convert to paid (60–80% trial conversion for B2B is achievable with follow-up)
- Month 4–6: Build referrals between orgs ("Which platform does your school use?")

## Viral and Referral Mechanics

### Activate the Existing Referral Engine
The referral programme is built but not marketed. Immediate actions:

1. **Email blast to all 500 users** explaining the referral programme
2. **In-app referral widget** on dashboard (not buried in a menu)
3. **Post-challenge share prompt:** "You just solved this in 3 minutes! Share your achievement — and earn Premium days when your friend joins"
4. **Leaderboard referral column:** Show how many referrals each user has made (competitive)

### New Viral Loops to Build

**Achievement Share Cards:** Auto-generate a shareable image when a user earns a top achievement ("I just hit Level 15 on PyLearn") — optimized for WhatsApp, Instagram Stories, Twitter. Each card has the PyLearn URL embedded.

**Weekly Battle Results:** After every 1v1 battle, generate a shareable "battle result card" with both players' names (optional) and the score. Winner naturally wants to share.

**Leaderboard Position Share:** "I'm ranked #7 in Nigeria this week on PyLearn" — one-click share to Twitter.

**Study Group Virality:** When a study group forms, each member gets a personalized invite link to share with their network. Group leaderboard creates external shareability.
