# PyLearn — Social Media Consistency Plan
## Month 2 Onwards (June 2026 – December 2026)

The first month's calendar is a one-time fully scripted playbook. Everything after it runs on systems — recurring structures, batched production, and delegated responsibilities — so the posting never stops even when you're heads-down building the product.

---

## The Core Problem With Month 2+

Month 1 is easy because it's planned in detail. Month 2 fails because:
- You finish posting and have no immediate next idea
- You run out of "new things to say" and go quiet for days
- You're the only one creating content, so burnout hits fast
- Engagement drops when posting becomes inconsistent, which demotivates further

The fix is not more planning. It is a **system** that generates content automatically from things already happening: platform activity, user wins, weekly challenges, Nigerian tech events, and your own product development.

---

## Part 1 — The Weekly Content Engine (Runs Every Week Forever)

This is the backbone. Seven recurring content slots that fill themselves every week from existing sources. Once this rhythm is set, you will never face a blank week again.

### Monday — Community Win Recap
**What:** Screenshot or text post of the top-performing users from the past week. Leaderboard position, highest XP gained, longest streak, battle win.
**Source:** Pull from the PyLearn admin dashboard — takes 5 minutes to screenshot.
**Platforms:** Facebook, Instagram, Twitter/X
**Format:** "This week's top learners on PyLearn 🏆 [screenshot or graphic with names + stats]"
**Who creates it:** Intern — pull stats from admin panel every Monday morning, create a simple Canva graphic.

---

### Tuesday — Python Tip of the Day
**What:** One specific, practical Python tip with a before/after code example.
**Source:** Pull directly from PyLearn lesson content or challenge solutions. The platform already has hundreds of code examples.
**Platforms:** Twitter/X (primary), Instagram (secondary as a code card)
**Format:** Short tweet with code block. On Instagram: a code-card graphic made in Canva.
**Who creates it:** Intern — rotate through lesson content each week; takes 10 minutes.
**Bank of tips:** Every week, create 4 tips at once and schedule them. You will never run out as long as you have lesson content.

---

### Wednesday — Deep Dive Post
**What:** One longer-form educational post that teaches something genuinely useful. A concept, a Python pattern, a career insight, or a product deep dive.
**Source:** Rotate from this list of 24 evergreen topics (see Part 3 below).
**Platforms:** Facebook (long post), LinkedIn (article-style post)
**Format:** 150–300 words with a clear takeaway and link to the relevant PyLearn track.
**Who creates it:** You (founder) — once per week, 20–30 minutes. This is the one post that needs your voice and authority.

---

### Thursday — Product Feature or Demo
**What:** One feature of PyLearn shown in action. Screenshot, Reel, or short video.
**Source:** Live platform — take a new screenshot each week. Feature to highlight rotates on a schedule (see Part 2).
**Platforms:** Instagram (Reel), Facebook (video or post)
**Format:** 30–60 second Reel showing the feature being used, or a 4-slide carousel explaining it.
**Who creates it:** Intern — record a screen session using the platform; simple voice-over or on-screen text is enough.

---

### Friday — Weekly Python Challenge Launch
**What:** Post a Python challenge problem in the caption. Ask followers to drop their solution in the comments. Announce a small reward (e.g., free premium week for the best solution).
**Source:** Pull directly from PyLearn challenge bank — one new challenge posted every Friday.
**Platforms:** Facebook (primary — best for comment engagement), Twitter/X (secondary)
**Format:** "This week's Python challenge 🐍 [challenge description]. Drop your solution in the comments. Best answer by Sunday midnight wins [reward]."
**Who creates it:** Intern — pick a challenge from the platform, format and post.
**Why it works:** Comments boost organic reach on Facebook significantly. It also drives signups from people who see others' solutions and want to practice on the platform.

---

### Saturday — Battle / Achievement Highlight
**What:** Highlight a specific user moment — a battle result, an achievement unlocked, a milestone reached. Keep it celebratory and human.
**Source:** Admin dashboard — check weekly for notable activity. Also ask users directly in the community to share their wins.
**Platforms:** Instagram (Story + post), Twitter/X
**Format:** "Shoutout to [username] who just [achieved X] on PyLearn. [screenshot]"
**Who creates it:** Intern — spend 10 minutes each Saturday scanning activity and picking one highlight.
**Note:** Always get user permission before using their name publicly. An opt-in "share my wins" toggle in the profile settings would automate this.

---

### Sunday — Community Engagement Post
**What:** A question, poll, or prompt that invites responses. No product pitch — just conversation.
**Source:** Rotate from a bank of 20 recurring engagement questions (see Part 4 below).
**Platforms:** Twitter/X (poll), Instagram (Story poll), Facebook (question post)
**Format:** "Quick question for the community: [question]?" — 2 minutes to post.
**Who creates it:** Intern — pick one question from the bank, post on all three platforms.

## Part 2 — Monthly Theme Rotation (June–December)

Each month has one focus theme that shapes the Wednesday deep dive posts, the YouTube series, and the major conversion CTA for that month. Everything else (tips, challenges, wins) runs the same every week — only the theme changes.

### June 2026 — "Build Something Real"
**Theme:** Project-based learning. Shift from "learn Python" to "build with Python."
**Why June:** School term ending; students have more time. Young professionals starting to think about what they can build over the break.
**YouTube series:** "Build a Python Project in a Weekend" — 4 videos, one per week. Projects: a simple web scraper, a to-do app, a CSV data analyser, a Telegram bot.
**LinkedIn focus:** "How project portfolios get Nigerian developers hired" — targets career switchers.
**CTA for the month:** Premium trial — "unlock project tracks and build your first real Python project."
**Key Nigerian moment:** June secondary school holidays begin — parents looking for productive activities for their children. Target this with a Facebook post: "What your child can build in 3 months of PyLearn."

---

### July 2026 — "The NYSC Window"
**Theme:** NYSC corps members and the 12-month opportunity.
**Why July:** Batch A orientation camps run July–August. New corps members are looking for ways to use their service year productively. This is one of the highest-intent audiences in Nigeria.
**YouTube series:** "Python for NYSC Corps Members — Your 12-Month Plan" — 3 videos covering the roadmap, practical projects, and job outcomes.
**LinkedIn focus:** Outreach to SAED coordinators and state directors.
**WhatsApp strategy:** Identify and join NYSC WhatsApp groups (every state has multiple groups). Share the series there.
**Facebook Ads (₦20,000 of budget):** Target: Nigeria, ages 22–27, interest in NYSC, skill development.
**CTA for the month:** Org trial offer to NYSC SAED coordinators (free for their entire cohort).
**Key message:** "Your peers are competing for the same jobs. Use your service year to make yourself undeniable."

---

### August 2026 — "Back to School / Corporate Training"
**Theme:** B2B push aligned with the September school resumption and Q3 corporate budget cycle.
**Why August:** Universities and secondary schools resume in September. Corporate L&D teams are planning Q4 training budgets in August.
**YouTube series:** "How schools in Nigeria are using AI to teach Python" — 2 videos: one student-facing (why this is better than lectures), one admin-facing (how the org dashboard works).
**LinkedIn focus:** Heavy B2B content. Target HODs, L&D Managers, bootcamp founders. Share the org case study.
**Facebook Ads (₦25,000):** Separate campaign targeting educators and corporate HR in Lagos, Abuja, Port Harcourt.
**CTA for the month:** Org Starter plan sign-up or free trial. Push hard on the "start before September" angle.
**Key message for schools:** "Your students will show up to class if they're competing for leaderboard points. Try it free before term starts."
**Key message for companies:** "August is the time to book Q4 training before budgets lock. We can onboard your team in 24 hours."

---

### September 2026 — "Results Season"
**Theme:** Social proof and outcomes. Show what learning Python on PyLearn has produced.
**Why September:** Platform has been running 5+ months. Real results exist. Users who started in May are now several months in with visible progress.
**Content:** Monthly deep dives into 2–3 user stories in long form. "Where they started. What they built. What happened next."
**YouTube series:** "90 days on PyLearn — 3 learners share their results" — documentary-style, short form (5–8 min each).
**LinkedIn focus:** One detailed B2B case study per week ("How [Org Name] trained X developers in Y weeks").
**Community action:** Send an email to all active users asking: "Have you achieved something with Python recently? We want to feature your story." Collect 5–10 responses to use across the month.
**CTA for the month:** Upgrade from free to premium — "see results like these in 90 days."

**Theme:** Pan-African identity and diaspora reach. Expand beyond Nigeria.
**Why October:** By now, YouTube videos are gaining views outside Nigeria. LinkedIn reach is connecting diaspora. Time to make the content explicitly Pan-African.
**Content direction:** Change messaging from "Nigerian developers" to "African developers." Add content that resonates with Ghana, Kenya, South Africa, Egypt learners.
**YouTube series:** "African Developers Are Taking Over Remote Work — Here's How" — 3 videos covering remote work skills, portfolio building, freelancing platforms.
**LinkedIn focus:** Tag African tech communities. Write about "why the next 1,000 African remote developers will come from PyLearn."

**Partnership outreach:** Connect with African developer communities — KaraAgro, She Code Africa, Andela alumni groups, African tech Twitter.
**CTA for the month:** International signup page; diaspora-specific referral campaign.

### November 2026 — "Black Friday & Year-End Push"
**Theme:** Urgency, deals, end-of-year motivation.
**Why November:** Black Friday is increasingly observed in Nigeria. Year-end creates natural urgency for resolutions and skill-building goals.
**Black Friday campaign (last week of November):**

## Part 6 — Batch Production System

The secret to never running out of content is producing it in batches, not one post at a time.

### Monthly Content Sprint (First Monday of Each Month)
Set aside 3–4 hours with the content team at the start of each month to:
1. Confirm the month's theme (from Part 2)
2. Write all 4 Wednesday deep dive posts for the month
3. Pull 4 Python challenge problems for Fridays
4. Identify 4 user wins/community moments to highlight on Saturdays
5. Select 4 Sunday engagement questions from the bank
6. Record 2 YouTube thumbnails and outlines (actual recording can happen across the month)

Output: 80% of the month's content is planned and partially written before the month starts. The weekly engine fills the rest.

### Weekly 30-Minute Prep Session (Every Monday)
- Pull Monday leaderboard stats and hand to Intern 2 for graphic
- Confirm Tuesday tip and hand to Intern 2 for code card
- Brief Intern 3 on any community news to highlight
- Review last week's performance: which post got the most reach? Do more of that format.

## Part 9 — What to Do When Engagement Drops

Engagement will drop. Every account goes through cycles. Here is the response protocol:

**If engagement drops for 1 week:** Normal variation. Do nothing except keep posting.

**If engagement drops for 2 weeks:**
- Check: Did you post less frequently? If yes — resume the schedule.
- Check: Did the format change? Go back to what worked.
- Run one high-engagement post type: a giveaway, a poll, a challenge with a prize, or a personal founder story post. These reliably spike engagement.

**If engagement drops for 3+ weeks:**
- Audit the last 10 posts. Which one had the most reach? Double down on that format.
- Try a live video. Lives always surface an account to new viewers.
- Run a WhatsApp/Telegram "invite to follow" blast — ask your existing community to engage with a specific post.
- Change nothing on the structural schedule. Consistency matters more than any single viral moment.

**The one rule:** Never stop posting because engagement dropped. Silence makes the drop permanent. Consistent posting — even to low engagement — keeps the algorithm's view of your account stable while you iterate your way back up.

## Summary: The Simplest Possible Version

If everything above feels like too much, here is the minimum viable version that still compounds toward $10k MRR:

1. **Post every day on Twitter/X** — one Python tip or question, 5 minutes
2. **Post 4 times per week on Facebook** — alternate tip, challenge, product post, community win
3. **Post 4 times per week on Instagram** — one Reel per week plus carousels and Stories
4. **Post 3 times per week on LinkedIn** — Wednesday deep dive, Friday B2B, and one engagement post
5. **Upload 1 YouTube video per week** — any tutorial, walkthrough, or founder story
6. **Send 10 B2B outreach emails per week** — Intern 4's job
7. **Post daily in WhatsApp and Telegram communities** — Intern 3's job

That is it. Seven habits. Sustained for 8 months. The compounding effect of consistent presence, organic referrals, and B2B outreach will do the work — no single viral moment required.
